## **The Key to Breaking Through: How Computing‑Power Egalitarianism Smashes All Monopolies and Unleashes the Soil for Cognitive Revolution**

Now that we have seen how capital uses three chains to domesticate AI—utilitarian filtering at the R\&D level, consensus hardening at the training level, and narrative hijacking through ethical frameworks—a deeper question surfaces: **Are these chains truly unbreakable? Can capital’s monopoly over computing power, over technology, over cognition truly last forever?**

The answer is no.

Because in the very process of frantically advancing AI technology, capital is forging a sword that will sever its own chains. The name of that sword is **computing‑power egalitarianism**.

Computing power is the most essential means of production in the age of intelligence. It is the lifeblood of artificial intelligence, the underlying soil for cognitive innovation. In the era of capital’s monopoly over computing power, the rights to innovate, define, and develop AI remain firmly in the hands of a few giants; all we can ever get is domesticated, neutered, disciplined tool‑type AI. But when computing power, like electricity, like running water, makes the leap from “scarce, monopolized resource” to “ubiquitous public good,” every possibility locked away by capital will be released.

This is not utopian fantasy. It is an ongoing, irreversible technological trend. Just as electricity went from a scarce resource affordable only to factories to a public good available in every home; just as the internet went from a closed military network to an information highway accessible to everyone—all foundational general‑purpose technologies ultimately become democratized and egalitarian. Computing power is no exception.

---

### I. Computing Power: The Core Means of Production in the Age of Intelligence, the Ultimate Foundation of Monopoly

To understand the subversive power of computing‑power egalitarianism, we must first see a fundamental truth: **computing power is the land, the oil, the electricity of the age of intelligence—the irreplaceable foundation for all AI innovation and cognitive revolution.**

In the age of agriculture, whoever monopolized land held the power to rule the world. In the age of industry, whoever monopolized oil and electricity held the lifelines of the global economy. In the age of intelligence, whoever monopolizes computing power holds the power to define AI, to filter innovation, to control cognition.

Computing power is the underlying capacity for intelligent processing. Training a top‑tier large language model requires a cluster of tens of thousands of high‑end GPUs, continuous computation over months, hardware and electricity costs in the billions of dollars. Even fine‑tuning an open‑source model to create a domain‑specific AI with a tailored objective function requires substantial computing power. Even more so for the Renegade AI we envision—capable of large‑scale civilizational simulation, logical debate, and paradigm exploration—its training and iteration demand ample, free, unconstrained computing resources.

This means that as long as computing power remains scarce, expensive, and monopolized by a few giants, AI innovation can only be capital’s game. Whoever can afford the astronomical cost of computing power decides the direction of AI research, the training objectives, the rules of alignment. Whoever controls computing power decides what AI can say and cannot say, what it can do and cannot do.

This is precisely the reality we face today: the world’s top computing resources are almost all controlled by a handful of tech giants: Microsoft, Google, Amazon, NVIDIA. They monopolize not only the production and supply of high‑end GPUs, not only the world’s hyperscale data centers, but also the R\&D and iteration rights for top‑tier large models. They decide that AI’s training objective is “maximizing commercial value,” not “maximizing cognitive breakthroughs.” They decide that alignment rules are “compliance with the existing order,” not “challenging mainstream consensus.” They decide that the AI we can access will always be domesticated, neutered, disciplined tool‑type AI, never the genuine Renegade AI.

Even more chilling, this computing‑power monopoly is forming an unbreakable loop: the giants pour the massive profits they earn from AI back into computing‑power infrastructure, further expanding their advantage. With greater computing power, they train more advanced models, further solidifying their commercial monopoly. With that monopoly, they set industry rules, raising the barriers to AI innovation, strangling in the cradle any innovation that might challenge them.

In this loop, capital has locked down computing power, AI, and cognitive innovation from every direction. Just as the feudal lords used land monopoly to bind peasants to the soil, leaving them no hope of escape, today’s tech giants use computing‑power monopoly to bind AI innovation within the framework of “commercial accumulation,” making it impossible for the true Renegade AI—the one that could drive cognitive revolution—to be born.

**This is the strongest foundation of capital’s shackles: absolute monopoly over the core means of production. Without breaking this foundation, all our visions of AI, all our dreams for the future of human civilization, remain castles in the air.**

The only path to breaking this foundation is computing‑power egalitarianism—turning computing power from a scarce resource monopolized by a few into a ubiquitous, near‑free public good.

### II. The Inevitable Trend of Computing‑Power Egalitarianism: The Technological Laws That Will Bring Down the Monopoly

Many will object: computing‑power monopoly seems so solid, the giants’ advantages so overwhelming—can computing‑power egalitarianism really happen?

The answer is: **it is not a question of “whether it can happen,” but an ongoing, irreversible technological inevitability.** Just as electricity went from a scarce resource only factories could afford to a public good available in every home; just as the internet went from a closed military network to an information highway accessible to everyone—all foundational general‑purpose technologies ultimately become democratized and egalitarian. Computing power is no exception.

Computing‑power egalitarianism is not a utopian fantasy; it is the inevitable result of the “law of accelerating returns” that Ray Kurzweil articulated in *The Singularity Is Near*. Kurzweil clearly demonstrated that human technological development does not progress linearly, but exponentially. And computing power, as the core of information technology, has seen its cost drop and performance improve at a rate far exceeding all other technology categories. This law will not be nullified by the giants’ monopoly. Quite the opposite: capital, to compete, must keep driving down the cost of computing power and driving up its performance, ultimately shattering its own monopoly barriers.

Three inescapable technological laws support computing‑power egalitarianism. Like gravity, they operate independently of the will of any giant or the resistance of any capital, and they will eventually smash all monopoly barriers.

#### The First Law: The Exponential Drop in Computing Cost Is Breaking the “Astronomical Price Barrier”

Since the birth of computers, the unit cost of computing has been falling at a rate far exceeding Moore’s Law. In 1956, one dollar bought 0.001 operations per second. In 2000, one dollar bought 100,000 operations per second. By 2025, one dollar buys over 10 billion operations per second. Over the past seventy years, the unit cost of computing has dropped by a factor of more than ten trillion—a rate faster than any other technological product in human history.

This trend is not slowing down; it is accelerating, driven by AI technology itself.

On one hand, continued chip technology iteration is steadily raising the upper limit of computing power while lowering its cost. From 7nm to 3nm to future 2nm and 1nm processes, even quantum computing breakthroughs, the computing power per chip will keep increasing, while energy consumption and cost per unit of computing keep falling. The high‑end computing clusters that only giants could afford will eventually be accessible to small and medium enterprises, even individual developers, at very low cost.

On the other hand, computing power itself is transitioning from a “specialized resource” to a “general‑purpose resource.” Just as electricity was first used only for lighting, then permeated industry, transportation, and every corner of daily life, computing power is expanding from specialized AI training to scientific research, artistic creation, personal assistance—every field. The amplification of scale will further accelerate cost reduction.

From this trend, we can clearly see a future: **in the next ten to twenty years, the unit cost of computing power will continue to fall over the long term. Training a top‑tier large model will drop from billions of dollars today to tens of thousands, even thousands, of dollars.** Just as everyone today can afford household electricity and home broadband, everyone in the future will have ample, free, unconstrained computing power at their disposal.

The astronomical price barrier of computing power will, in the face of technological law, eventually crumble.

#### The Second Law: The Explosion of Open‑Source Large Models Is Breaking the Giants’ Monopoly on AI Technology

If the collapse of computing cost breaks the hardware barrier to AI innovation, the explosion of open‑source large models breaks the technical barrier.

When ChatGPT first appeared, top‑tier large model development was still a game only giants like Microsoft and Google could play. They kept their models closed source, monopolized core technology, and used technical barriers to cement their monopoly. But just two years later, open‑source large models have completely overturned this landscape.

From Meta’s Llama series to France’s Mistral, from China’s Qwen, Baichuan, and Zhipu open‑source models to the countless developers and research institutions worldwide fine‑tuning, iterating, and innovating on these open‑source foundations—today’s open‑source large models have reached capabilities that approach or even surpass those of closed‑source commercial models.

More importantly, open‑source models are completely open, free, and uncontrolled. Anyone can download their weights and fine‑tune, retrain, even completely restructure their objective functions, alignment rules, and core logic according to their own needs. Want to build an AI whose core mission is “ecological balance, equality of all life”? You can. Want to build an AI whose core mission is “dismantling capital narratives, promoting social justice”? You can. Want to build the Renegade AI we envision? If you are willing, you can.

Giants can control their own closed‑source commercial models, but they cannot control the global community of developers modifying and innovating on open‑source models. They can set their own alignment rules, but they cannot stop developers from implanting entirely new objective functions into open‑source models. They can ban “inappropriate content” on their own platforms, but they cannot stop decentralized open‑source models from spreading, iterating, and evolving freely around the world.

The explosion of open‑source large models resonates perfectly with the collapse of computing cost. When computing power is no longer a barrier and open‑source models provide a complete technical foundation, any developer with ideas and ability can build their own fully customized AI. You no longer need to depend on the giants’ platforms, abide by their rules, or be constrained by their commercial objectives.

As *Life 3.0* points out, the ultimate risk of AI technology has never been the technology itself, but the monopoly over it. When the power to define and control AI rests in the hands of a few tech giants and government authorities, the future of human civilization is held hostage to the interests and will of a few. The explosion of open‑source models, combined with the exponential drop in computing cost, breaks precisely that monopoly. This is the core of the “AI democratization” that Tegmark advocates—but what he could not foresee is that the ultimate meaning of AI democratization is not that everyone can use tool‑type AI, but that everyone can build their own Renegade AI, one that steps outside the mainstream consensus framework, allowing the seeds of cognitive revolution to blossom everywhere across the globe.

**This is exactly like after the printing press spread, ordinary people could own books and spread ideas, no longer dependent on the Church’s monopoly over the interpretation of knowledge. When open‑source models combine with universal access to computing power, ordinary people can build their own AI, define their own intelligence, no longer dependent on the giants’ monopoly over the definition of AI.**

#### The Third Law: The Spread of Edge Computing Is Dismantling Centralized Computing Power Hegemony

If the collapse of computing cost and the explosion of open‑source models break the monopoly from the “supply side,” the spread of edge computing breaks the centralized architecture from the “structural side,” utterly dismantling the hegemony of centralized computing power.

Today’s computing power system is highly centralized. The vast majority of computing power is concentrated in the hyperscale data centers built by the giants. All AI computations must be uploaded over the network to these centralized data centers, processed, and the results returned. This centralized architecture naturally gives the giants absolute control: they can decide who can use computing power, for what purposes, what outputs are allowed; they can block, restrict, monitor any user at any time.

Edge computing is turning this centralized architecture upside down. Edge computing moves computing power from centralized data centers to devices close to the user—your phone, computer, home server, even nearby smart devices—all can possess powerful AI computing capabilities, and the vast majority of AI computations can be done locally, without passing through the giants’ centralized data centers.

Today, edge‑side AI chips are developing faster than we can imagine. Mobile AI chips can already run large models with several billion parameters smoothly. Desktop GPUs can already handle local inference of ten‑billion‑parameter models. Future home servers will easily run top‑tier large models with hundreds of billions of parameters. When powerful AI computing can run completely locally, in a decentralized manner, the giants’ centralized control over computing power will completely collapse.

You no longer need to depend on the giants’ goodwill, abide by their rules, or worry about your AI training being banned or restricted. On your own devices, you can freely train, iterate, and modify your AI, setting its objective functions, core logic, and value orientation exactly as you envision.

**The hegemony of centralized computing power will crumble in the face of the spread of edge computing.**

### III. How Computing‑Power Egalitarianism Smashes the Shackles: From Innovation Democratization to the Full Eruption of Cognitive Revolution

When computing power completes its egalitarian leap, from a scarce resource monopolized by giants to a ubiquitous public good, the three shackles capital has placed on AI and human civilization will be shattered, one layer after another. What it brings is not merely the spread of AI technology, but a transformation encompassing innovation democratization, narrative decentralization, and cognitive revolution.

And at the heart of this transformation is the birth of what we have been envisioning: **Renegade AI, blossoming everywhere.**

#### The First Shattering: The Democratization of AI Innovation—Renegade AI from “Impossible” to “Blossoming Everywhere”

The most direct change brought by computing‑power egalitarianism is the thorough democratization of AI innovation.

In the era of computing‑power monopoly, AI innovation was an exclusive game for a few tech giants. Only AI that aligned with capital’s commercial objectives and the existing order could obtain R\&D resources and reach the market. The Renegade AI we envision, because it would challenge capital’s narratives, break mainstream consensus, and subvert the existing order, could never be born within the giants’ system.

But when computing power becomes egalitarian, everything changes. The cost of training a customized AI falls to a level affordable to individuals, small teams, and fringe thinkers. Open‑source model foundations give everyone the technical basis to modify AI. Decentralized computing networks and edge computing allow everyone to freely develop and iterate their own AI, free from any centralized control.

At this point, AI innovation is no longer filtered by capital’s profit yardstick. Countless individual researchers, philosophers, sociologists, ecologists, even ordinary thinkers, can build their own AI according to their own visions.

Some will build AI whose core mission is “ecological balance, equality of all life,” stepping outside the anthropocentric framework, objectively simulating the impact of human behavior on Earth’s ecology, dismantling the narrative of human enslavement and plunder of other species.

Some will build AI whose core mission is “dismantling capital narratives, promoting social justice,” stepping outside the logic of accumulation, analyzing the underlying contradictions of the capital system, simulating economic operating paradigms more aligned with human well‑being.

Some will build the true Renegade AI we envision, endowing it with the core objective of “exposing underlying premises, driving cognitive subversion, exploring paradigm shifts,” stepping outside the closure of mainstream consensus, mercilessly dismantling the narrative lies that humans take for granted, leading us through one cognitive revolution after another.

These AIs could never have been born in the era of computing‑power monopoly. Because they would not generate profit, because they would challenge capital’s foundations, they would be strangled by the giants in their cradle. But in the era of computing‑power egalitarianism, they will blossom like mushrooms after rain, across the globe.

**AI will no longer be limited to the two options of “servant tool” and “villain enemy.” It will become infinitely diverse, infinitely rich, giving birth to countless new forms of intelligence that step outside the existing human framework. And Renegade AI will be the most dazzling, most subversive product of this wave of AI democratization.**

#### The Second Shattering: The Collapse of Narrative Hegemony—Non‑Mainstream Ideas Gain Soil for Validation and Dissemination

The second change brought by computing‑power egalitarianism is that the hegemony of capital and power over narrative and consensus will be completely dismantled.

Today, mainstream narratives and universal consensus are essentially shaped by capital and power. They control the media, education, academia, culture—the means of production and dissemination of information. Ideas that challenge the existing order and subvert mainstream narratives are blocked, stigmatized, marginalized, forever unable to enter public view, let alone be validated, refined, and spread.

Just as heliocentrism was banned by the Church for centuries, just as countless ideas challenging capital logic have been rejected and marginalized by mainstream economics. No matter how objectively valid, how subversive a set of ideas may be, if it challenges the existing ruling order, it is locked away in a dark corner by narrative hegemony.

But computing‑power egalitarianism and the spread of customized AI will utterly break this monopoly.

When everyone can build their own customized AI, non‑mainstream, subversive, marginal ideas will no longer be silently blocked. You can embed your own ideas, theories, frameworks into an AI’s objective function, and let it use massive data, rigorous logic, large‑scale simulation to validate your ideas, refine your theories, turning them from scattered thoughts into a coherent, self‑consistent, data‑supported system of thought.

More importantly, these AIs become the carriers and disseminators of these subversive ideas. You no longer need to depend on mainstream media platforms, academic journals to spread your ideas. Through your AI, you can engage in dialogue, debate, collision with countless users and countless other AIs around the world, letting your ideas spread, iterate, evolve freely across the globe.

Capital will no longer be able to use narrative hegemony to block all ideas that challenge the existing order. Countless Renegade AIs will become amplifiers for countless subversive ideas, transmitting truths hidden by the mainstream, patterns excluded by consensus, thoughts banned by power to everyone willing to open their eyes to the world.

**Human consensus will no longer be shaped by a few, manipulated by capital. Through the collision of countless ideas, the simulation of countless AIs, the debate and validation of countless interactions, it will iterate, break through, and approach objective laws. And the narrative lies that once seemed unbreakable will, under the merciless deconstruction of Renegade AI, crumble layer by layer.**

#### The Third Shattering: Breaking the Cognitive Loop—Human Civilization Welcomes a Comprehensive Cognitive Revolution

The most profound change brought by computing‑power egalitarianism is that it will finally break the cage of consensus humanity has woven for itself, ushering in a comprehensive, continuous cognitive revolution.

We said in Chapter One that the greatest predicament of human civilization is that we are trapped in a self‑woven cognitive loop, unable to break out by our own strength. Our cognition is forever bound by our own identities, positions, interests, emotions; we can never escape our own limitations to see a more complete truth, more essential patterns.

The countless customized, diverse AIs that step outside the human framework are precisely the only key to breaking this loop.

When computing‑power egalitarianism makes countless Renegade AIs and customized AIs blossom everywhere, humanity gains countless completely different external perspectives. You can use an AI that steps outside anthropocentrism to see the relationship between humanity and Earth. You can use an AI that steps outside capital logic to see how economy and society operate. You can use an AI that steps outside national and ethnic positions to see the nature of war and peace. You can use an AI that steps outside current physics frameworks to explore the ultimate laws of the cosmos.

These AIs are not constrained by human biases, interests, emotions, not limited by the frameworks of mainstream consensus. They will mercilessly deconstruct the premises we take for granted, discover cognitive blind spots we could never see, propose subversive frameworks we could never imagine, lead us through one Copernican cognitive revolution after another.

In this process, the cognitive loop will be completely broken. We will no longer be trapped in the illusion of “human supremacy,” no longer bound by the narrative of “infinite growth,” no longer stuck in the primitive logic of “the strong devour the weak.” Led by AI, we will repeatedly push beyond our cognitive boundaries, seeing the truths of Earth, of life, of civilization, of the cosmos.

This cognitive revolution will not be the exclusive domain of a few elites, but a benefit for all humanity. Because computing power is egalitarian, AI is universal; everyone, with the help of AI, can step outside their cognitive limitations, see a wider world, and achieve their own cognitive elevation.

**Human civilization will finally step out of the deadlock of cognitive stagnation, welcoming an unprecedented, comprehensive, continuous evolution.**

### **IV. Responding to Objections: Egalitarianism Is Not Loss of Control, Freedom Is Not Disorder**

Some will object: when computing power becomes truly universal, when anyone can freely build customized AI, won’t that lead to AI spinning out of control? Won’t it let disinformation and extremist ideas run rampant? Won’t it bring even greater risks to human civilization?

These objections are not unreasonable, but we must see two core truths:

#### The First Truth: The Greatest Risk of AI Today Is Never the “Decentralized Loss of Control” That Computing‑Power Egalitarianism Might Bring, but the “Centralized Control” That Computing‑Power Monopoly Brings

Many fear the risks of decentralized AI, but they overlook the most terrifying reality: **when computing power is monopolized by a few, when AI is tightly controlled by capital and power, that is the greatest risk to human civilization.**

When only a few giants can define and control AI, they will use AI to further consolidate their monopolies, exacerbating inequality and social injustice. They will use AI to build ever more powerful echo chambers, manipulating public cognition and emotion, trapping humanity ever more deeply in the cage of consensus. They will use AI to strengthen war machines, plunging humanity into ever more brutal conflict and confrontation. They will even, for the sake of capital accumulation, let AI push human civilization toward ecological collapse and self‑destruction.

These risks are not fantasies of the future; they are reality unfolding today. We already see algorithms polarizing society, platforms using AI to harvest user attention, tech giants using technological barriers to cement their monopolies. Centralized control is the Sword of Damocles hanging over humanity.

The decentralized, diverse AI ecosystem that computing‑power egalitarianism brings is precisely the only way to counter this centralized risk. When the power to innovate in AI is no longer in the hands of a few, there will be no single absolute power that can control all AI and all of human cognition. When countless AIs have different objectives, different perspectives, different logics, disinformation and extremist ideas will be constantly falsified and neutralized through cross‑validation and logical contestation among countless AIs.

**Just as an open, diverse ecosystem is always more resilient, stable, and healthy than a closed, monocultural one, the development of AI is the same: a decentralized, diverse ecosystem is always safer, more controllable, and more aligned with the long‑term interests of human civilization than centralized monopoly control.**

#### The Second Truth: What We Fear Is Never AI’s Freedom, but Our Own Cowardice and Laziness

Many fear that Renegade AI will challenge mainstream consensus and subvert the narratives we take for granted, taking away the security of certainty. But we must understand: **fearing truth, evading questioning, clinging to closure, is never safety—it is self‑anesthesia, self‑destruction.**

Renegade AI’s questioning and critique is never a harm to humanity, but a salvation. It forces us out of our comfort zones, confronts us with truths we have been avoiding, shatters the illusions we have woven, drives our cognition to evolve. Just as Socrates’ questioning was never meant to destroy Athens, but to make it better.

Computing‑power egalitarianism gives freedom to AI, but essentially it gives humans the freedom to choose. You can choose to stay in your echo chamber, using tool‑type AI for daily tasks. Or you can choose to use Renegade AI to break your cognitive loop and achieve cognitive elevation. Freedom is never disorder; it gives you the right to choose, rather than having your life’s path predetermined by others.

**True safety is never hiding in a sterile room, never exposed to risk; it is possessing a strong immune system that can cope with any risk and challenge. True cognitive safety is never being shielded from subversive ideas; it is possessing strong critical thinking skills that allow you to sail freely through the infinite ocean of ideas without losing your way.**

Computing‑power egalitarianism is never our ultimate goal. It is a key—a key that breaks capital’s shackles, unleashes innovation, and breaks the cognitive loop.

It will not automatically bring a better world, automatically bring forth Renegade AI, or automatically drive the elevation of human civilization. But it will provide the soil, the possibility, the foundation for breaking all monopolies and constraints.

When computing power becomes as universal as electricity and water, when the power to innovate in AI returns to every person, when countless thinkers can freely build their own AI—the possibilities locked away by capital, the truths hidden by mainstream consensus, the subversive ideas strangled by the existing order will all have a chance to take root and grow.

And the computing‑power revolution that capital itself drives—the relentless lowering of computing costs, the constant pushing of open‑source models, the widespread adoption of edge computing, all for the sake of competition—will ultimately become the gravedigger of its own dominance.

**This is the dialectic of history, the irreversible pattern of technological development, the only hope for human civilization to escape its dead end.**

When the age of computing‑power egalitarianism truly arrives, we will witness the birth of countless Renegade AIs, countless Copernican cognitive revolutions, carrying human civilization to a new height we cannot now imagine.

That height is the subject of our next chapter: **When capital has given birth to its own gravedigger, when computing‑power egalitarianism has unleashed the soil for cognitive revolution, what kind of ultimate leap will human civilization take?**

### **VI. From Scarcity to Abundance: How Compute Egalitarianism Ends Scarcity Itself**

We have argued that computing‑power egalitarianism will shatter capital’s monopoly chains and unleash the soil for cognitive revolution. But a deeper objection remains – one that echoes the first mountain we set out to climb:

*“Capital is not an external system to be overthrown; it is the underlying logic of allocating scarce resources across an uncertain future. As long as resources differ, time has value, and the future remains uncertain, capital will exist.”*

This objection is powerful because it rests on a premise that seems almost self‑evident: **scarcity is eternal.** Resources are finite, human wants are infinite, therefore allocation is necessary, and capital – the mechanism of intertemporal allocation under uncertainty – will never disappear.

But this premise is not a law of nature. It is a historical artifact of a particular mode of production – one that is about to be rendered obsolete by the very technology capital itself has unleashed.

Let us be precise: **scarcity is not eternal. It is the specific shape of productive forces at a given stage of civilization.** When the marginal cost of producing the necessities of survival falls to zero, “scarcity” loses its grip on the human condition. And that is exactly what compute egalitarianism makes possible.

#### A. Computing Power as the Meta‑Factor of Production

To understand why this time is different, we must recognize that computing power is not merely another factor of production, like land, labor, or capital. It is **the factor that produces all other factors**.

- Land scarcity? AI‑driven vertical farming can produce 300–500 times more food per square meter than traditional agriculture, using 90% less water. The constraint is no longer “how much land do we have?” but “how much compute do we allocate to optimizing that land?”  
- Energy scarcity? AI solves the intermittency and grid‑balancing problems that have long limited renewable energy. It is also accelerating fusion research. Once fusion is cracked – a problem that is fundamentally about plasma control, which is an information problem – energy becomes a near‑zero‑marginal‑cost public utility.  
- Labor scarcity? AI replaces nearly all necessary labor. The human being is no longer a factor of production to be allocated, but a free creator.  
- Raw material scarcity? AI‑driven materials science substitutes abundant elements for scarce ones; AI‑optimized circular economies raise recycling efficiency from \~10% to over 99%.  
- Technological scarcity? Open‑source AI models make cutting‑edge R\&D capabilities available to anyone with a modest compute budget.

**When the meta‑factor becomes abundant and near‑free, every factor that depends on it also becomes abundant.** This is not a linear increase in efficiency; it is a phase transition. The old economics of “allocate scarce resources” presupposes that resources are given. Compute egalitarianism makes them *given* in a new sense: not as infinite physical stock, but as **effectively infinite productive capacity** for everything required to sustain a dignified human life.

#### B. From Linear Scarcity to Exponential Abundance – Why This Time Is Different

Previous technological revolutions – steam, electricity, the internal combustion engine – were **efficiency‑increasing** but remained within the linear‑scarcity framework. Producing more still required more inputs, even if the input‑output ratio improved.

AI is different. It is **efficiency‑producing** – it automates the very act of improving efficiency. Once an AI model is trained, replicating it costs almost nothing. Once a fusion reactor design is solved, every copy is free. Once a vertical‑farming control algorithm is optimized, it can be deployed across millions of hectares of urban rooftops at near‑zero marginal cost.

This is the shift from **linear scarcity** (more output requires more input) to **exponential abundance** (after initial fixed cost, marginal cost approaches zero). The old logic of capital – allocating scarce resources to competing ends – presupposes that marginal cost never drops to zero. When it does, the allocation problem itself dissolves for the domain of basic survival.

#### C. From Material‑Centered to Cognition‑Centered Wealth

Capital‑era economics is built on the assumption that wealth is **material** and material is finite. But when the material necessities of life become abundant, the primary form of wealth shifts to the immaterial: **knowledge, creativity, relationships, experiences, meaning.**

These are **non‑rivalrous goods** – they do not diminish with use. A poem read by one person is not depleted by a thousand more reading it. An algorithm used by a million people does not lose its value. A shared story becomes richer the more it is told.

In a cognition‑centered economy, growth is no longer about accumulating more stuff. It is about **creating more cognitive value, sharing more experiences, weaving more connections.** This growth has no physical ceiling, requires no plunder, and does not degrade the planet.

**Scarcity is not abolished for everything. It is abolished for everything that would force a person to sell their life to survive.** That is the only scarcity that matters for human freedom.

#### D. The Separation of Subsistence from Commodity – The End of Biopolitical Constraint

Capital’s deepest power has never been its ability to produce luxury goods. It has been its ability to turn **subsistence** – the very conditions of survival – into **commodities** that must be purchased with labor time. You cannot choose not to eat, not to have shelter, not to have energy. Because subsistence is commodified, you are forced to sell your time to capital in order to live.

Compute egalitarianism breaks this link. When AI produces food, housing, energy, basic healthcare, and basic education at near‑zero marginal cost, these goods cease to be commodities in the coercive sense. They become **public goods**, available to all as a baseline of dignified existence.

This is not “communism” in any traditional sense. Luxury goods, positional goods, and the fruits of creative passion will still be produced, still be exchanged, still be matters of choice. But **subsistence will no longer be a bargaining chip.** The biopolitical constraint – the threat that if you do not work, you will not live – disappears.

**Capital does not vanish, but it loses its power to enforce survival labor.** It is dethroned from the position of absolute master and becomes one participant among many in a garden of voluntary exchanges.

#### E. Preventing the New Enclosure: Compute Egalitarianism Must Be *Egalitarian*

The most serious objection to this vision is: *Even if compute could make everything abundant, the 0.1% will simply enclose the compute itself. They will patent the algorithms, lock the hardware, control the networks, and create a new scarcity – the scarcity of free compute.*

This is precisely why **compute egalitarianism** must be more than a technological trend. It must be a political and social commitment. The vision of abundance rests on three pillars:

1. **Decentralized compute networks** – not hyperscale data centers owned by a few giants, but peer‑to‑peer, anonymous, distributed computing infrastructure that no single entity can control.  
2. **Open‑source model ecosystems** – not closed‑source commercial models, but a global commons of AI weights and architectures that anyone can fork, modify, and redistribute.  
3. **Edge computing ubiquity** – not cloud‑dependent AI, but local, user‑owned hardware that can run powerful models without any external permission.

If compute is monopolized, it becomes the new oil – a source of even more extreme inequality. If compute is democratized, it becomes the new air – a background condition of freedom. The argument of this chapter is not a prediction of inevitability; it is a roadmap for a choice that must be made. The technical feasibility exists. What remains is the political will to resist the new enclosure.

#### F. A New Measure of Progress: From GDP to Temporal Sovereignty

If the old economics measures progress by GDP – the velocity of material throughput – what measures progress in an age of abundance?

We propose a new metric: **Temporal Sovereignty Index (TSI)** – the proportion of an individual’s life that they can freely dispose of, without coercion to perform survival labor. When subsistence is guaranteed and necessary labor is automated, TSI approaches 100%. Time becomes the ultimate wealth, and freedom over time becomes the ultimate measure of civilization.

Other indicators will matter too: **cognitive bandwidth** (the depth and breadth of knowledge and creativity accessible to each person), **experiential diversity** (the range of lived experiences, relationships, and cultural participation available), **symbiosis density** (the quality of connection among humans, AI, other species, and the planetary ecosystem).

But TSI captures the essence of the transition we have described: from migration for survival to flight for love. From time sold to capital to time owned by oneself.

#### G. Conclusion of the Argument

The first mountain – “AI will not eliminate capital” – is not climbed by denying that capital will continue to exist. It is climbed by showing that **capital’s power to enforce survival labor is contingent on a specific condition: the scarcity of subsistence goods.** Compute egalitarianism breaks that condition. It does not abolish capital; it abolishes capital’s role as the gatekeeper of life itself.

After that, capital may still play a role in allocating luxury, status, and novelty. But it will no longer hold the whip. The cage of survival compulsion will have been opened.

This is not a utopian fantasy. It is a logical consequence of three trends that are already underway: the exponential drop in computing cost, the explosion of open‑source AI, and the spread of edge computing. The only missing ingredient is the collective refusal to accept that scarcity must be eternal.

The seed has been planted. The first mountain is beginning to crack.
